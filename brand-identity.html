02 — The Mark

Built From
First Principles

The Archetype mark is a synthesis of two forces in tension: the arc of creative possibility and the structural certainty of the "A" form. Together, they encode the studio's dual mandate — insight and expression, strategy and craft.

Geometric, modular, and designed to hold its integrity at every scale — from a favicon to a billboard, from monochrome to full colour.

The Arc

A crescent in ascent — representing creative vision, the horizon of possibility, and the illuminating power of insight.

The Peak

The structural A-form — architectural, directional, grounded. The ambition to build something that lasts.

The Curve

A sweeping base that grounds the mark — suggesting momentum, forward motion, and a brand in full stride.

The mark is always used from source SVG files. It is never recreated, distorted, outlined, or recoloured outside the approved variants. Minimum digital size: 24px. Minimum print size: 8mm.

Hierarchy

Deep Space is the canvas. Electric Blue leads all active states and CTAs. Magenta punctuates — use sparingly for maximum impact. Red and Blue are logo-specific variants, not UI colours.

Gradients

Combine Blue → Cyan for data and flow contexts. Magenta → Violet for campaign and emotional moments. Never exceed two accent hues in a gradient. Full spectrum for hero sections only.

Accessibility

All body text must achieve WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1). Electric Blue on Deep Space passes at large sizes only. Always test body copy contrast before final output.

06 — Graphic Language

Signature Devices

ARCH
Chromatic Glitch

Cyan, magenta, and red channel separation applied to typography or the logomark. Signals digital-native edge. Use for campaigns, social content, and cultural moments. Reserved for impact — never overused.

Neon Glow

Electric colour applied as luminous light sources on dark fields — as if surfaces are emitting rather than reflecting light. Applied to the logomark and UI components in premium digital contexts.

The Horizon Line

A luminous horizontal line — the boundary between the known and the possible. Used as a section divider, compositional anchor, and spatial device in presentations and environmental applications.

Light Trails

Flowing, curved light paths suggesting speed, energy, and momentum. Always on dark backgrounds. Created through long-exposure photography or vector simulation.

Grid Overlay

Fine dot or line grids at 3–6% opacity create depth behind logomark or typographic compositions. Never compete with foreground content.

Radial Burst

Circular rings of colour-segmented light used as full-frame abstract imagery or graphic punctuation. Electric colour only — never warm tones.

07 — Photography & Imagery

The World
After Dark

Archetype's world is nocturnal and electric. Our imagery operates in the space between the organic and the technological — where neon light falls on natural forms, where screens glow in empty rooms, where human connection happens under dramatic, purposeful light.

The visual palette is always: dark base, electric accent, cinematic framing. No harsh sunlight. No stock photography clichés. No natural daylight as primary context.

08 — Brand Voice & Tone

Words That
Mean Something

The Archetype voice is the verbal equivalent of the visual identity: sharp, purposeful, and quietly confident. We don't oversell. We say what we mean, precisely.

01
Purposeful

Every sentence earns its place. No filler, no fluff. If it doesn't add meaning, it doesn't make the cut.

02
Intelligent

We write for smart audiences. We don't over-explain or dumb down. Complexity is respected, not avoided.

03
Confident

Declarative, not tentative. We make statements. Hedge words — 'perhaps', 'maybe', 'might' — are minimised.

04
Human

Behind every brief is a person. We write with warmth and directness — never corporate, never robotic.
